02

The Problem

Existing tools demanded too much setup before delivering value. Teams were spending more time managing their tools than doing actual work. Onboarding drop-off was at 61% within the first week — a clear signal the experience was broken from the start.

Before

Average 14 taps to create a task. Nested menus, mandatory fields, no sensible defaults. Teams abandoned the app within days.

Insight

Users didn't need more features — they needed fewer decisions. The interface was optimising for flexibility at the cost of clarity.

Research synthesis / affinity map
03

The Approach

We ran 24 contextual interviews across three industries, shadowing teams during their actual workdays. Every friction point was catalogued. From there, we ran a design sprint to rapidly prototype and validate three distinct interaction models before committing to a direction.

The winning concept centred on a single-input philosophy — one text field that intelligently parses intent. Type a task, and Forma infers the assignee, deadline, and priority from natural language.

"It felt like the app was reading my mind — I never had to explain myself twice."
— Beta user, 6-week trial
04

The Solution

A radically simplified interface built on three principles: speed of capture, clarity of ownership, and honest progress tracking. We stripped the UI to its essential components and introduced a persistent command bar that works across the entire app.

05

The Outcome

Launched in Q3 2024 to 12,000 beta users. Within 8 weeks, weekly active usage grew by 180%. The redesigned onboarding cut drop-off from 61% to 18%. Customer support volume dropped by 68% — the interface had become self-explanatory.

Forma is now the primary productivity tool for over 400 remote-first teams, with an App Store rating of 4.8 and a Net Promoter Score of 72.
